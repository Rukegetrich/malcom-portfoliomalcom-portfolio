### Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment**: This does not read as AI slop overall. The five case studies each use a genuinely distinct visual recipe (browser-chrome bleed, floating device composite, a deliberately "quiet" typographic layout, split-column, centered cinematic video) rather than one component looped five times — real variation, not a template. No glassmorphism, no gradient text, no identical card grids, no hero-metric cliché. The numbered 01/05–05/05 sequence is earned (five real, different projects), not reflexive scaffolding.

**Deterministic scan**: The CLI pass on the source file and the live browser overlay together surfaced **32 anti-pattern occurrences** across these rules:
- `repeated-section-kickers` — **6 instances**: "Selected Work", "About", "Experience", "How I Lead", "Skills & Tools", "Get In Touch". This is the skill's own named #1 tell ("tiny uppercase tracked eyebrow above every section") and the detector caught it even though it doesn't tip the site into reading as AI-made overall — worth a conscious decision either way, not silent repetition.
- `nested-cards` — **3 instances**: `.case-visual-contained`, `#about.dark-band`, `#experience.curtain-sheet` (card-in-card nesting, an explicit ban).
- `layout-transition` — **6 instances** across two passes: `max-width` (logo hover-expand), `padding-left` (skill-list hover), `width`/`height` (the custom cursor's dot and ring, twice each). Animating these properties instead of `transform`/`opacity` risks jank.
- `line-length` — multiple paragraphs/list items running **89–197 characters per line**, well past the 65–75ch guidance (up to ~2.6x over in the worst case).
- `tiny-text` — 11px used not just for short mono labels but for full descriptive sentences (`.case-context`).
- `all-caps-body` — long uppercase spans up to 72 characters (the hero eyebrow line), which hurts legibility at length.
- `tight-leading` — 1.05 line-height on `.soft` spans.
- `overused-font` / `codex-grid-background` — both borderline: the Fraunces/Inter split and the faint background grid-line texture read as a deliberate, disciplined 2-font system and an intentional atmospheric detail (used sparingly, bookending hero + footer) rather than generic AI defaults. Flagging for visibility, not as real problems.
- `numbered-section-markers` (advisory, no line number) — resolved by the LLM review: this is a real 5-case sequence, not scaffolding. Not a violation.

**Visual overlay**: Injection succeeded on the live site and produced a findings overlay (ticker bar + on-page highlight boxes), captured via screenshot as evidence. ### Overall Impression
A confident, editorially art-directed portfolio that avoids the generic-AI-template look almost everywhere it matters — the five distinct case recipes and the "tension" callout in each case are real differentiators for a senior-IC hiring audience. It falls short in the places recruiters actually touch first: the mobile nav is unreadable by default, the very first case study breaks its layout on a phone, and several text blocks run far past a comfortable reading measure. The biggest opportunity is closing the gap between the desktop craft (which is genuinely strong) and the mobile experience (which currently undercuts it).

### What's Working
1. **The "tension" callout in every case** ("What we tried first," "The constraint we had to name," "The hard call") demonstrates trade-off judgment — exactly what a senior-design hiring manager screens for, not just polished outcomes.
2. **Typographic discipline**: Fraunces (display) / Inter (body) / JetBrains Mono (labels) is applied with real consistency — mono never leaks into body copy, serif never leaks into meta.
3. **Five distinct case-study recipes** avoid the identical-card-grid AI tell; the site reads like an art-directed editorial spread, not a looped component.

### Priority Issues

**[P1] Mobile primary nav text is functionally invisible.** `.nav-overlay nav a` sits at `opacity: 0.14` with only a `:hover` rule to raise it to `1`. Below 980px this overlay is the *only* navigation (the desktop nav-links are hidden), but touch devices have no persistent hover state — confirmed via computed style. Why it matters: a recruiter on a phone can't read the one navigation menu the site gives them. Fix: raise the baseline opacity to ~0.75–0.85 and gate the dim/brighten hover trick behind `@media (pointer: fine)`, matching how the ambient cursor system already restricts itself.
**Suggested command**: `/impeccable adapt`

**[P1] Case-number wraps into three broken lines on mobile for the flagship case studies.** `.case-meta-top` is `flex-wrap: nowrap` with no `white-space: nowrap` / `flex-shrink: 0` on `.case-number`, so at 375px "01 / 05" wraps into "01" / "/" / "05" stacked vertically — confirmed on both Regulon (the very first case study) and Dormot. Why it matters: this is the first craft impression a mobile recruiter gets scrolling into the flagship project, and it looks unfinished. Fix: add `white-space: nowrap; flex-shrink: 0;` to `.case-number`/`.case-industry`, or stack `.case-meta-top` in a column below ~480px.
**Suggested command**: `/impeccable adapt`

**[P1] Line-length and text size badly overshoot readability guidance.** Multiple paragraphs/list items run 89–197 characters per line (vs. the 65–75ch target), and `.case-context` renders full descriptive sentences at 11px rather than reserving that size for short mono labels. Why it matters: long, tiny lines increase reading effort exactly where the case-study narrative is doing the most persuasive work. Fix: cap prose blocks to the site's own `--w-read: 720px` token (it exists but isn't applied everywhere), and bump `.case-context` to 13–14px or shorten it to a true label.
**Suggested command**: `/impeccable typeset`

**[P2] Nested-card pattern in three places.** `.case-visual-contained`, `#about.dark-band`, and `#experience.curtain-sheet` all put a bordered/shadowed surface inside another bordered/shadowed surface — an explicit anti-pattern. Fix: flatten one layer in each case (usually the outer "curtain" container can lose its own shadow/radius once the inner element already carries one).
**Suggested command**: `/impeccable layout`

**[P2] Several CSS transitions animate layout-triggering properties instead of transform/opacity.** Six occurrences: `max-width` (nav-logo hover-expand), `padding-left` (skill-list hover), and `width`/`height` (the custom cursor's dot and ring, each transitioning both properties). Why it matters: these can cause layout thrash/jank, especially the cursor system since it runs on every pointer move. Fix: swap to `transform: scale()` for the cursor dot/ring sizing, and `max-width`→`grid-template-columns`/`clip-path` or accept a fixed max-width with opacity-only reveal for the nav-logo expand.
**Suggested command**: `/impeccable optimize`

### Persona Red Flags

**Jordan (recruiter skimming quickly)**: No repeatable scan pattern across cases — role/tools metadata sits in a different visual position in every recipe, costing re-orientation time on each case. The triple "03/05" on BravePay is a small but real signal against "detail-oriented," which undercuts the pitch for a senior design hire.

**Casey (distracted mobile user)**: Hits the broken "01 / 05" wrap on Regulon within the first screen scrolled past the hero — an immediate "is this finished?" moment. If she taps the menu button instead of scrolling, she's greeted by nearly invisible link text (14% opacity) with no visible indication anything is tappable.

**Sam (accessibility-dependent user)**: Skip link works correctly. The nav overlay is marked `role="dialog"` but has no Escape-key binding and no visible focus trap — a keyboard user can open it but nothing stops tabbing into the hidden page behind it. The Stemscope `<video autoplay muted loop>` has no exposed pause control — a WCAG 2.2.2 gap even though it's short, muted, and decorative.

### Minor Observations
- BravePay's header prints "03 / 05" three times in one screen (case-number, q-meta, and the preceding breath separator) — delete the redundant one in `.q-meta`.
- BravePay and Pot of Food both link "View more work on Behance →" to the generic profile grid, while Regulon/Dormot link to live product sites and Stemscope links to its exact case page — a recruiter expecting more detail lands on an undifferentiated grid instead.
- Case-industry tag punctuation is inconsistent: middle-dot ("Fintech · Compliance SaaS") vs. comma-separated ("Consumer , Food Delivery , AI").
- 5 nav items plus a ~17,000px total scroll is slightly over the ≤4-choices guideline, and the one wayfinding aid that offsets the length (section-index) disappears below 1180px.
- Print stylesheet for recruiters saving as PDF, and `schema.org/Person` markup on the footer, are both genuine, uncommon technical polish worth keeping.

### Questions to Consider
- What if all five case studies placed role/tools/industry metadata in exactly the same visual slot, so the varied imagery/composition alone carries the "distinct recipe" idea without costing scan-pattern consistency?
- What if the mobile nav's dimmed-link treatment were strictly a desktop hover-discovery device, and touch got a fully legible list by default — would that single change measurably shift how much a mobile recruiter trusts the rest of the site's craft?
- Six repeated eyebrow-kickers is exactly the pattern this skill flags as the most common AI tell — was that a deliberate systemic choice for this site, or worth varying the cadence on at least one or two sections?
