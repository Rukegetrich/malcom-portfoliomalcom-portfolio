01 / 05 Fintech · Compliance SaaS

B2B SaaS platform automating KYB onboarding and fraud detection for financial services teams

Regulon

regulon.io / onboarding / kyb-verification

What if compliance onboarding felt as clear as anything else people sign up for?

Role Senior Product Designer Tools Figma, FigJam, Maze
Problem

Financial operations teams were abandoning KYB onboarding mid-flow, manual document review and unclear compliance steps created friction that eroded trust at the most critical moment.

What we tried first

The first design used a wizard flow, a step-by-step progress bar where each screen was isolated. Testing revealed that users wanted to see the full scope of what was required before starting, not be led through it blindly. We reversed the approach: show the whole journey upfront, let users jump between sections.

Process
  • Rebuilt AML/KYC onboarding as a guided, step-aware flow, not a static form
  • Simplified multi-step wallet and compliance workflows across web and mobile
  • Built a reusable design system eliminating duplicate UI patterns across the product
60%+Less manual processing time
25%Higher onboarding completion
40%Faster dev implementation

02 / 05 Proptech · Web & Mobile

Proptech startup building property management software for real estate operators across Africa

Dormot

Four tools, zero coordination. Property operators in Africa needed one place that actually worked.

Role Product Designer Tools Figma, FigJam, Notion
Problem

African real estate operators were managing property listings, guest access, and short stays across four disconnected tools, with no single source of operational truth.

The constraint we had to name

Early designs tried to serve property operators and tenants equally from a shared interface. Mid-project we surfaced the tension: designing for two audiences simultaneously was diluting the experience for both. We made the explicit decision to prioritise operators as the primary user, tenants became a simpler, separate experience. It was a hard conversation but it unlocked the design.

Process
  • Designed onboarding and operational workflows spanning mobile and web in one product
  • Ran discovery workshops to surface real operator needs over internal assumptions
  • Maintained a scalable, documented design system to eliminate repetitive effort

BravePay

03 / 05 Fintech · Mobile Wallet

Mobile-first digital wallet and payment platform built for SMEs in the African market

03 / 05 Senior Product Designer Sketch · Figma · InVision

BravePay needed to help African SMEs send, receive, and verify payments digitally, without the anxiety that most fintech compliance flows create at the exact moment trust is most fragile. The challenge wasn't technical. It was perceptual.

Problem

Compliance-heavy onboarding was colliding with the need to feel simple and welcoming for first-time digital wallet users. Every additional verification step introduced doubt, not security.

What we built

A wallet and KYC flow that surfaced verification requirements only when they became relevant, not as an upfront gate. Compliance happened in the background of the experience, not in front of it.

The hard call

The original KYC flow had nine steps. Testing showed abandonment at step three, not because the steps were unreasonable, but because users couldn't see where it ended. We removed five steps entirely and introduced a single progress marker. The first version of this felt like giving something up. It turned out to be the right call.

+25% Lift in checkout conversion after reducing the verification flow from nine steps to four, and repositioning compliance as context rather than a gate.

04 / 05 Consumer · Food Delivery · AI

AI-assisted food ordering and discovery app launched in the Nigerian consumer market

Pot of Food

What if you could order exactly what you want, not just what the menu allows?

Role Senior UI/UX Designer Tools Figma, InVision, Miro
Problem

Existing food ordering apps locked users into rigid menus with no room to customise, a real barrier for communities with specific food cultures and preferences.

Direction we abandoned

We built discovery around cooks posting available dishes. Users kept trying to specify what they wanted before ordering. The content model was backwards. We flipped it two weeks before handoff.

Process
  • Designed an AI-assisted discovery and customisation flow for meal ordering
  • Built onboarding focused on accessibility, simplicity, and first-order retention
  • Iterated on ordering clarity using direct customer behaviour and feedback

Hardware and UX project: a low-cost DIY VR headset designed for underserved Nigerian schools

My Stemscope

Reimagining practical STEM education without requiring a science lab.

Problem

Design decision

The original concept was a full virtual lab environment. Research quickly revealed the real challenge wasn't immersion, it was accessibility, affordability, and offline usability.

Role Product Designer Tools Figma, Adobe XD
Process
